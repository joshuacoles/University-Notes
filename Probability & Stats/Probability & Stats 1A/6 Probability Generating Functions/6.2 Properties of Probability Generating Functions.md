The $k$th moment of a random variable $X$ is $ğ”¼(X^{k} )$.
Typically we are interested in the first two moments,
$ğ”¼(X)$ and $ğ”¼(X^{2} )$ as from these we can

Theorem 28 (Generating the $k$th factorial moment)

$G_{X}(s )$. Then

(a) 

$G_{X}(1 )=1$.

(b)

$$

(a) 

$$

(b)

$$
\begin{align}
